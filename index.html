Perfect Tenses

Present Perfect: A tense used to describe actions or situations that started in the past and are still relevant or continue to the present.
Past Perfect: A tense used to describe actions that were completed before another action in the past.
Future Perfect: A tense used to describe actions that will be completed by a certain time in the future.

Tense Rule Example
Present Perfect Subject + has/have + past participle She has visited Paris twice.
Past Perfect Subject + had + past participle By the time I arrived, the movie had already started.
Future Perfect Subject + will have + past participle By next week, I will have finished my project.
